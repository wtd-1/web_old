William T. Doan is a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science student at the University of Texas at Dallas (UTD) in the Erik Jonsson School of Engineering .


Mr. Doan's research interests revolve around computer networks. In particular, his current work focuses on generating accurate network trace data for ad hoc networks, especially in evolving topologies and intermittent connectivity. This work is conducted with Dr. Brian Ricks serving as Mr. Doan's research advisor. Previously, Mr. Doan did research under Dr. Yapeng Tian. Under Dr. Tian, Doan conducted research in audiovisual novelty detection, audiovisual sound-source segmentation, and object detection according to joint attention at looking targets.


Presently, Mr. Doan's research interests include:
network data generation   delay/disruption tolerant networks    influence maximization   large-scale networks
distributed construction of connected dominating sets   constant-factor approximation    bayesian networks
network structure learning   constructing connected dominating sets under uncertainty
