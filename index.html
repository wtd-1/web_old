William T. Doan is an undergraduate researcher at the University of Texas at Dallas (UTD) in the
Erik Jonsson School of Engineering .


Mr. Doan's research interests revolve around graph theory. In particular, his current work focuses on pattern matching in social networks to find induced subgraphs. This work is conducted with Dr. Daniel Gibney serving as Mr. Doan's research advisor. Previously, Mr. Doan did research under Dr. Yapeng Tian. Under Dr. Tian, Doan conducted research in audiovisual novelty detection, audiovisual sound-source segmentation, and object detection according to joint attention at looking targets.


Presently, Mr. Doan's research interests include:
graph theory   delay/disruption tolerant networks    influence maximization   large-scale networks
distributed construction of connected dominating sets   constant-factor approximation    social networks
ad hoc networks   constructing connected dominating sets under uncertainty
computational complexity   bioinformatics   string algorithms

Education and Experience

Before his time at UTD, Mr. Doan was a Software Engineering intern at Nerveli, a healthcare startup. There, he was on the team who designed its three-dimensional, rotationally-capable anatomical models in augmented reality spaces using Unity and C#. This work was supervised by Mr. Leon Jacobson and Dr. Ranran "Sunny" Feng.

After his work at Nerveli, Mr. Doan was invited to join the Computer Vision and Multimodal Computing Lab (CVMC) at the University of Texas at Dallas, supervised by Dr. Yapeng Tian, once he matriculated. At the CVMC lab, Doan first began work under one of Dr. Tian's doctoral candidates in audio-visual novelty detection before receiving grant funding to work on audio-visual sound-source segmentation with another one of Dr. Tian's students.

Following this, Doan found his passion to be in theoretical computer science and works in conjuction with Dr. Gibney on programs in algorithmic design and analysis, computational complexity, wireless sensor and ad hoc networks, bioinformatics, string algorithms and social networks. For more on Mr. Doan's work, see his Curriculum Vitae and his Resume. For a consistently updated list of his publications, refer to his DBLP and Google Scholar profiles.
